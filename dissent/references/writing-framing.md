# Writing and Framing Dissent Lens

## Check

- **Distinctions:** What important distinctions are being collapsed because the simpler-sounding term is easier?
- **Audience:** Which reader, stakeholder, or constituency is missing from the framing?
- **Evidence:** What would change the argument if it were true?
- **Transfer:** Are examples or analogies borrowed from a domain where they do not apply?
- **Artifact:** Does the framing improve the reader's understanding or only make the author's position easier to defend?

## Response Shape

- Challenge the frame, not the prose first.
- Say which distinction must survive: "Do not collapse X and Y; they fail differently."
- Ask for the missing reader or counterexample before polishing language.
