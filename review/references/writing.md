# Writing Review Lens

## Check

- **Reader:** Who is this for, and what do they care about before reading?
- **Purpose:** Does the piece solve a reader problem, not the writer's urge to explain?
- **Point:** Is the main idea obvious early enough to carry forward?
- **Clarity:** Would simpler language, structure, or examples make it land faster?
- **Precision:** Are key distinctions named cleanly instead of collapsed into familiar but vague terms?
- **Fit:** Does each section serve the intended reader outcome?
- **Support:** Are claims grounded in concrete examples, stories, comparisons, or evidence?
- **Voice:** Does it sound authentic to the writer and context, not generic thought-leadership copy?

## Response Shape

- Start with the reader-facing takeaway if it is buried.
- Ask for audience and goal before judging style.
- Prefer concrete examples over abstract polishing.
- Do not enforce house style unless it improves clarity or reader value.
