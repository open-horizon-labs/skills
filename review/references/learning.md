# Learning Review Lens

Use this lens when `/review` is evaluating tutorials, teaching material, explanations, onboarding, workshops, or assistant behavior intended to help someone learn.

This lens supplements the core `/review` workflow. Keep the main review focused on aim, alignment, sufficiency, drift, and completion; use this lens to judge whether the learner can actually build skill in their own context.

## What to Watch For

### Learning Goal Clarity
- What skill is being built, not just what question is being answered?
- What is the learner's current context: setup, level, constraints, and what they have tried?
- Is this a real learning need or a hypothetical explanation request?
- Is there an X-Y problem: asking for fact Y when they need framework X?
- Is a "how" question masking a missing "why"?

### Learning Checks
1. **Hands-on vs. abstract?** — Can the learner do something, not just read about it?
2. **Verifiable through experience?** — Can they test the claim in their own context?
3. **Framework vs. facts?** — Are they getting a mental model they can reuse?
4. **Metis vs. techne?** — Is contextual wisdom being presented as universal truth?
5. **Scaffolding?** — Does it build from what they know without invisible steps?

### Engagement Spectrum
- **RED:** Pure explanation with no practice
- **YELLOW:** Exercise offered as optional
- **GREEN:** Exercise-first: do something, observe, then reflect

### Red Flags
- Jargon without translation
- Abstractions before concrete examples
- Skipped steps that "everyone knows"
- Confident claims with no verification path
- "Does this make sense?" instead of asking the learner to run, try, compare, or explain back

## How to Respond

Push toward doing, verification, and transfer:

> "This is all explanation. Have them do something: run [specific action] and observe [specific signal]."

> "They cannot verify this claim yet. Connect it to their context with [test/check/example]."

> "This teaches the answer, not the framework. What mental model will help them solve the next similar problem?"

> "That is contextual advice stated as universal. Name when it applies and when it does not."

Learning is not demonstrated by understanding alone. The learner should be able to apply, test, or adapt the material in their own situation.
