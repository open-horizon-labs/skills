# Learning Review Lens

## Check

- **Skill:** What capability is the learner building, not just what answer are they receiving?
- **Context:** What is their setup, level, constraint, and prior attempt?
- **Frame:** Is there an X-Y problem or a missing "why" behind the "how"?
- **Practice:** Can the learner do something, observe a result, and adjust?
- **Verification:** Can they test the claim in their own context?
- **Transfer:** Are they getting a reusable mental model, not isolated facts?
- **Simplicity vs. ease:** Does it distinguish what is unfamiliar from what is actually complex?
- **Scaffolding:** Are steps sequenced from what they know, without invisible jumps?

## Response Shape

- Convert explanation into action: "Run X and observe Y."
- Tie claims to the learner's context with a test, check, or example.
- Separate contextual wisdom from universal rules: say when advice applies and when it does not.
- Avoid "Does this make sense?"; ask the learner to try, compare, or explain back.
